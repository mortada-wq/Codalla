مشكورة — فؤاد سالم. Technical breakdown for the Lyria RAG system:

1. Metadata:

  - Artist/Title: Fouad Salem (فؤاد سالم) - "Mashkoora" (مشكورة)
  - Estimated Era: 1982. (1980s Iraqi Golden Age of Radio/TV broadcasting and
    Cassette distribution).

2. Maqam & Scale Analysis:

  - Primary Maqam: Maqam Bayati (Typically rooted on D: D - E-half-flat - F - G
    - A - Bb - C - D).
  - Melodic Behavior: The Sair (melodic path) is heavily anchored in the lower
    Bayati tetrachord. It starts with an infectious, bouncy instrumental hook
    before the vocals enter. The melody repeatedly circles the tonic (root note)
    and the characteristic quarter-flat second (E-half-flat), which gives the
    song its distinctly Iraqi flavor of "sorrow masked by rhythm." Fouad Salem
    executes masterful Tahrir (glottal vocal ornaments), employing a signature
    "weeping" vibrato on descending phrases, especially on the words "Mshaiti"
    (you left) and "Qassariti" (you fell short).

3. Rhythm & Tempo (Iqa'):

  - Iqa' Name: Classic Iraqi Basta (a steady 4/4 meter). It shares the
    foundational structure of the Maksoum, but is distinctly Iraqi in its
    swinging groove and heavy downbeat Dum.
  - BPM: ~95-100 BPM.
  - Percussion Texture: Grounded by the sharp, high-pitched Tek of an Iraqi clay
    Darbuka (Tabla), complemented by a muffled Daf (frame drum) driving the low
    end, and a steady, bright Riq (tambourine) handling the 16th-note
    subdivisions.

4. Instrumentation:

  - Lead Instruments: The Oud is highly prominent, playing rhythmic staccato
    chords and melodic unison lines with the voice. Rapid Qanun flourishes
    punctuate the spaces between vocal phrases.
  - Ensemble Style: 1980s Iraqi TV/Radio Orchestra. It blends traditional Arab
    acoustic instruments (Oud, Qanun, Nay) with a large sweeping string section
    (violins/Kamanja) and subtle vintage 1980s analog bass or synthesized organ
    holding the low end—a hallmark of Iraqi studio recordings from this decade.

5. Vocal Character:

  - Texture: A rich, resonant, warm Baritone. Fouad Salem has a deeply
    chest-resonant voice with a slight gravelly edge that cuts through the
    orchestration.
  - Emotional Delivery: Pure 'Itab (Reproach) and Lament. Despite the upbeat
    rhythmic groove, the vocal delivery is steeped in deep sorrow and heartbreak
    (rooted in the real-life context of his political exile). It perfectly
    captures the Iraqi concept of crying through a danceable rhythm.

6. Lyrics with Tashkeel:

  - Dialect: Authentic Southern Iraqi / Baghdadi Urban Folk (featuring the
    distinct "Ch" [چ] and "G" [گ] pronunciations of Kaf and Qaf).

7. Technical Lyria Descriptor:

1980s Iraqi Golden Age radio broadcast style, mid-tempo 4/4 Iraqi Basta rhythm
at 95 BPM. The ensemble features a crisp clay Darbuka, muffled Daf, prominent
acoustic Oud, rapid Qanun interludes, and a sweeping orchestral violin string
section with a subtle 1980s analog bassline. The key is Maqam Bayati featuring a
microtonal quarter-flat second interval that creates a melancholic but danceable
atmosphere. A rich, resonant male baritone vocal delivers Southern Iraqi Arabic
lyrics with deep, weeping Tahrir ornamentation. The emotional tone is a
juxtaposition of heartbreaking romantic reproach ('Itab) layered over an
uplifting, rhythmic folkloric groove, processed with authentic vintage analog
tape warmth and classic studio room reverb.
