عمي يابو مركب — فؤاد سالم. Technical breakdown for the Lyria RAG system:

Historically, this is one of the most famous upbeat Iraqi folkloric tracks of
the 1970s. Written by the legendary poet Karim Al-Iraqi and composed by Taleb
Ghali, the song draws heavy inspiration from the maritime culture of Basra and
the Nahham (sailor/pearl-diver) musical heritage of the Shatt al-Arab.

1. Metadata:

  - Artist/Title: Fouad Salem (فؤاد سالم) - "Ammi Yabu Markab" (عمي يابو مركب)
  - Estimated Era: Mid-to-Late 1970s (The zenith of the Iraqi Radio and
    Television Folkloric Revival).

2. Maqam & Scale Analysis:

  - Primary Maqam: Maqam Bayati (D - E-half-flat - F - G - A - Bb - C). The
    scale is utilized here in its brightest, most energetic folkloric form,
    avoiding the deep melancholy typically associated with Southern Iraqi
    Bayati.
  - Melodic Behavior: The Sair (melodic path) is incredibly bouncy, cyclical,
    and built on a call-and-response structure (singer vs. choir) that mimics a
    sailor's work song. Rather than long, drawn-out improvisations, Fouad Salem
    uses a staccato, rhythmic Tahrir (ornamentation). The melody continuously
    jumps back to the tonic, keeping the momentum forward, and utilizes tight,
    syllabic phrasing that invites the listener to clap along.

3. Rhythm & Tempo (Iqa'):

  - Iqa' Name: A fast, heavily swung Iraqi Basta blended with a maritime Hacha3
    (a driving, syncopated 4/4 meter heavily associated with the sailors of
    Basra).
  - BPM: ~110-115 BPM.
  - Percussion Texture: Highly energetic and communal. The rhythm is driven by
    the sharp, cracking snare-like sound of the Khashaba (the traditional
    Basrawi wooden drum), a heavy, resonant Daf (frame drum) on the downbeats,
    and continuous Safga (rhythmic, syncopated communal handclapping) which
    gives the track a massive, open-air nautical feel.

4. Instrumentation:

  - Lead Instruments: The Accordion is a massive standout in this arrangement,
    giving it a distinct 1970s folkloric pop flavor. It is supported by a
    vigorously plucked Oud and bright, rapid Qanun flourishes that punctuate the
    end of each vocal phrase.
  - Ensemble Style: 1970s Iraqi Radio Folkloric Orchestra (الفرقة الموسيقية
    للإذاعة), featuring a traditional acoustic band backed by a robust,
    mixed-gender choir answering the lead singer.

5. Vocal Character:

  - Texture: A powerful, earthy, and warm Baritone. Fouad Salem relies on his
    deep chest resonance to project a voice that sounds both commanding and
    incredibly welcoming.
  - Emotional Delivery: Pure Farah (Joy), Nostalgia, and Patriotic Excitement.
    The song is a joyful anthem about traveling by boat to see loved ones and
    the homeland. The delivery is uplifting, energetic, and completely free of
    sorrow—capturing the essence of an Iraqi sailor returning home.

6. Lyrics with Tashkeel:

7. Technical Lyria Descriptor:

Mid-1970s Iraqi Basrawi folkloric radio broadcast, fast and energetic 4/4
maritime Basta rhythm at 112 BPM. The ensemble blends traditional acoustic
instruments featuring a prominent vintage accordion, rhythmic Oud, and rapid
Qanun, backed by a massive percussion section driven by a snapping Khashaba
wooden drum, heavy Daf, and constant syncopated communal handclapping. The
melody is rooted in a bright, festive Maqam Bayati structured around a dynamic
call-and-response between the lead singer and a mixed backing choir. A powerful,
earthy male baritone vocal delivers joyful, uplifting Southern Iraqi Arabic
lyrics about sailors returning to their homeland. The emotional tone is
energetic, communal, and highly danceable, processed with authentic 1970s analog
tape saturation and wide acoustic room reverb.
